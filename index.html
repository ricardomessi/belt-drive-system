    Physics Reference — Formulas Used in this Calculator
    01
    Belt Linear Velocity
    v = π · Deff · n / 60 000
    v [m/s] · Deff [mm] · n [RPM]
    Higher RPM or larger crankshaft pulley diameter produces a faster belt. All span tensions scale with belt speed.
    02
    Span Effective Tension
    Teff = PkW × 1000 / v
    T [N] · P [kW] · v [m/s]
    Power drawn by each accessory creates a tension difference across the pulley. Faster belt at same power means lower tension.
    03
    Hub Force Components
    Fx = Tin·cos θin + Tout·cos θout
    Fy = Tin·sin θin + Tout·sin θout
    The two incoming and outgoing belt spans each pull the pulley shaft. Their vector sum is the hub bearing load.
    04
    Resultant Hub Force
    Fhub = √( Fx² + Fy² )
    F [N] · Direction θ = atan2(Fy, Fx)·180/π [°]
    This is the actual force the belt exerts on the bearing. Exceeding the bearing rated load causes premature failure.
    05
    Speed Ratio
    SR = DCRK,eff / Dpulley,eff
    Pulley RPM = Engine RPM × SR
    The ratio of crankshaft to driven pulley diameter determines how fast each accessory spins relative to engine speed.
    06
    Gear-Driven Load on CRK
    ΔT = Pgear × 1000 / vbelt
    Torque τ = P / ω · ω = 2π·n/60 [rad/s]
    Gear-driven accessories (oil pump, water pump etc.) load the crankshaft directly, adding to the tight-side belt tension and increasing CRK hub force.

    📐 FEAD Physics Reference
    Capstan EquationTtight / Tslack = eμθMax tension ratio before slip. μ = friction coeff, θ = wrap angle (rad). Higher wrap → more grip.
    Slip Safety FactorSF = ln(Ttight/Tslack) / (μθ)SF <1.0 → slip. Target SF >1.3. Increasing tension raises SF but increases bearing loads.
    Belt Bending LossPbend = Cb · (Tavg/R) · vHysteresis per pulley. Small R or high tension increases losses. Aramid cord reduces Cb by ~30%.
    Centrifugal TensionTc = mb · v² / LReduces effective clamping at high speed. Aramid (low mass) minimises. Critical above ~20 m/s.
    Bearing Friction LossPbear = μb · Fhub · ω · RProportional to hub load and speed. Reducing hub load directly reduces bearing losses & extends L10 life.
    FEAD Efficiencyη = Paccessories / PCRK,totalTarget >96%. Losses: slip + bending + bearing + centrifugal. Aramid + optimal tension achieves 97–98.5%.
